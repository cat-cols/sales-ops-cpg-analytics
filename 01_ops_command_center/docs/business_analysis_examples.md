## Store Performance Deep Dive

### Case Study: Store #47 (Downtown) — Underperformance Analysis

**Executive Question:** "Why is Store #47 consistently missing its sales targets despite high foot traffic?"

#### Performance Overview

| Metric | Store #47 | Company Average | Variance |
|--------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Net Sales (Monthly) | $89,200 | $115,800 | -23% |
| Gross Margin % | 42.1% | 44.8% | -2.7 pts |
| In-Stock Rate | 67% | 89% | -22 pts |
| Sales per Labor Hour | $142 | $198 | -28% |
| Customer Count | 2,340 | 2,890 | -19% |

#### Root Cause Analysis

**Primary Issue: Inventory Stockouts**
- **Top 10 SKUs out of stock**: Average 4.2 days/month vs. 0.8 days company average
- **Lost sales impact**: Estimated $12,400 monthly from stockouts alone
- **Customer conversion**: 18% lower than average (customers leaving empty-handed)

**Contributing Factor: Staffing Gaps**
- **Labor hours vs. sales**: Store is overstaffed for current sales volume (28% above optimal ratio)
- **Staff utilization**: Only 62% of scheduled shifts align with peak traffic periods
- **Labor cost impact**: $4,200 monthly overspend on misaligned staffing

**Secondary Issue: Assortment Mismatch**
- **SKU performance**: 40% of inventory consists of slow-moving items (<2 units/month)
- **Space utilization**: High-demand SKUs allocated only 35% of shelf space
- **Local demand signals**: Downtown customer preferences skew toward premium products (under-represented)

#### Business Impact

**Monthly Financial Impact:**
- Lost sales from stockouts: $12,400
- Excess labor cost: $4,200
- Margin erosion from mix: $2,100
- **Total monthly opportunity cost: $18,700**

**Customer Impact:**
- Estimated customer defection rate: 8% higher than average
- Repeat purchase rate: 22% below company benchmark
- Customer satisfaction score: 3.2/5 vs. 4.1/5 average

#### Recommendations

**Immediate Actions (0-7 days):**
1. **Emergency replenishment**: Redirect $8,000 of inventory from Store #33 (overstocked) to Store #47
2. **Staffing realignment**: Adjust schedule to align 80% of shifts with peak traffic (12-6pm, Thu-Sat)
3. **SKU swap**: Replace bottom 20 SKUs with top-performing downtown-preferred products

**Short-term Actions (8-30 days):**
1. **Assortment optimization**: Conduct local market study to refine product mix
2. **Inventory review**: Implement dynamic replenishment based on sell-through rate
3. **Staff training**: Focus on inventory management and customer conversion techniques

**Long-term Actions (30-90 days):**
1. **Space reallocation**: Increase premium product allocation from 35% to 55% of shelf space
2. **Performance incentives**: Tie manager bonus to in-stock rate and conversion metrics
3. **Customer retention**: Implement loyalty program to recover defected customers

**Expected Improvement:**
- Net sales: +$15,200 within 60 days
- Gross margin: +1.8 points
- Labor productivity: +25%
- Customer satisfaction: +0.8 points

### Case Study: Store #12 (Highway 99) — Margin Erosion Analysis

**Executive Question:** "Why is Store #12's gross margin significantly below average despite strong sales volume?"

#### Performance Overview

| Metric | Store #12 | Company Average | Variance |
|--------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Net Sales (Monthly) | $134,500 | $115,800 | +16% |
| Gross Margin % | 36.3% | 44.8% | -8.5 pts |
| Average Discount Rate | 18.2% | 8.4% | +9.8 pts |
| Units Sold | 8,420 | 6,890 | +22% |
| Order Count | 1,890 | 1,650 | +15% |

#### Root Cause Analysis

**Primary Issue: Excessive Discounting**
- **Discount pattern**: Store manager applying unauthorized discounts averaging 18.2%
- **Discount justification**: Claims "competition pressure" but market analysis shows no price war
- **Margin impact**: $11,300 monthly margin erosion from excessive discounts alone

**Contributing Factor: Volume-over-Margin Mindset**
- **Sales incentive structure**: Store manager compensated on revenue, not margin
- **Customer behavior**: Customers now expect discounts, reducing willingness to pay full price
- **Competitive positioning**: Store competing on price rather than value differentiation

**Secondary Issue: Cost Structure**
- **COGS ratio**: 68% vs. 61% average (higher supplier costs due to smaller order quantities)
- **Operating expenses**: 12% above average (inefficient operations, not passed to customers)

#### Business Impact

**Monthly Financial Impact:**
- Margin lost to discounts: $11,300
- COGS inefficiency: $3,200
- Operating expense overrun: $2,400
- **Total monthly margin erosion: $16,900**

**Strategic Impact:**
- Brand positioning: Risk of being perceived as "discount store"
- Customer expectations: Difficult to reverse discount culture
- Market dynamics: May trigger price war with actual discount competitors

#### Recommendations

**Immediate Actions (0-7 days):**
1. **Discount approval workflow**: Implement manager approval required for discounts >10%
2. **Performance metric change**: Shift manager compensation from revenue to gross margin
3. **Customer communication**: Begin value-based messaging (quality, service, convenience)

**Short-term Actions (8-30 days):**
1. **Competitive analysis**: Document actual competitor pricing to dispel "pressure" myth
2. **Supplier negotiation**: Consolidate orders to reduce COGS by 3-4 points
3. **Staff training**: Train team on value-selling techniques vs. discount-focused selling

**Long-term Actions (30-90 days):**
1. **Brand repositioning**: Emphasize premium service and product expertise
2. **Loyalty program**: Implement value-based rewards (not just discounts)
3. **Cost optimization**: Review operations to reduce expense ratio to company average

**Expected Improvement:**
- Gross margin: +6.5 points within 60 days
- Net sales: -5% (acceptable trade-off for margin improvement)
- Customer retention: Stable (value-focused customers will remain)
- Brand perception: Shift from "discount" to "value"

### Case Study: Store #33 (Westside) — Labor Productivity Analysis

**Executive Question:** "Why is Store #33's labor cost so high despite strong sales performance?"

#### Performance Overview

| Metric | Store #33 | Company Average | Variance |
|--------|-----------|----------------|----------|
| Net Sales (Monthly) | $128,900 | $115,800 | +11% |
| Labor Cost | $24,800 | $19,100 | +30% |
| Labor Cost % of Sales | 19.2% | 16.5% | +2.7 pts |
| Sales per Labor Hour | $142 | $198 | -28% |
| Headcount | 12 | 9 | +33% |

#### Root Cause Analysis

**Primary Issue: Overstaffing Relative to Sales Patterns**
- **Staffing schedule**: Based on historical peak from 2023 (sales patterns have shifted)
- **Traffic patterns**: Peak hours now 2-3 hours later than scheduled (manager not updated schedule)
- **Staff utilization**: Only 58% of scheduled hours align with actual customer traffic

**Contributing Factor: Inefficient Labor Allocation**
- **Task distribution**: 40% of staff time on non-customer-facing tasks (backroom, inventory)
- **Cross-training gap**: Staff cannot flex between roles during slow periods
- **Scheduling rigidity**: Fixed schedules vs. flexible staffing based on demand

**Secondary Issue: Management Practices**
- **Micromanagement**: Manager personally handles tasks that should be delegated
- **Time tracking**: No accountability for how staff time is actually spent
- **Performance metrics**: No productivity goals or monitoring

#### Business Impact

**Monthly Financial Impact:**
- Excess labor cost: $5,700
- Opportunity cost (could reinvest in sales): $3,200
- **Total monthly inefficiency: $8,900**

**Operational Impact:**
- Staff morale: High (overstaffed) but low engagement (underutilized)
- Customer service: Inconsistent (staff not available during actual peak times)
- Management bandwidth: Wasted on scheduling rather than strategic initiatives

#### Recommendations

**Immediate Actions (0-7 days):**
1. **Schedule audit**: Align staffing with current traffic patterns (shift peak coverage to 3-8pm)
2. **Task analysis**: Identify non-customer-facing tasks that can be consolidated or eliminated
3. **Cross-training plan**: Begin training staff on multiple roles for flexibility

**Short-term Actions (8-30 days):**
1. **Flexible scheduling**: Implement demand-based staffing (reduce headcount by 2 FTEs)
2. **Productivity tracking**: Implement time-tracking to understand actual labor utilization
3. **Management coaching**: Train manager on delegation and strategic time management

**Long-term Actions (30-90 days):**
1. **Performance incentives**: Tie manager bonus to labor productivity metrics
2. **Technology investment**: Consider workforce management system for optimized scheduling
3. **Process redesign**: Streamline backroom tasks to free staff for customer-facing roles

**Expected Improvement:**
- Labor cost %: Reduce to 16.8% (near company average)
- Sales per labor hour: Improve to $185
- Headcount: Reduce to 10 FTEs
- Monthly savings: $5,700

## Variance Analysis — Beyond Flagging

### Sales vs. Budget Variance: Q1 2026

**Variance:** +$127,000 (Net Sales vs. Budget)

#### Traditional Flagging Approach
- **Status:** Variance > 5% threshold
- **Action:** Flag for review
- **Outcome:** "Investigate variance"

#### Business Value Approach

**Variance Decomposition:**

| Driver | Variance Impact | % of Total Variance | Root Cause |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|------------|
| Wholesale channel strength | +$89,000 | 70% | New distributor partnership |
| Retail channel weakness | -$34,000 | -27% | Store #47 and #12 underperformance |
| Price/mix effect | +$18,000 | 14% | Favorable product mix shift |
| One-time events | +$14,000 | 11% | Promotional calendar alignment |
| **Net Variance** | **+$87,000** | **68%** | **Explained** |
| Unexplained variance | +$40,000 | 32% | Further investigation needed |

**Business Narrative:**

The positive sales variance is primarily driven by **unexpected wholesale channel strength** (+$89K) resulting from a new distributor partnership that exceeded onboarding targets by 40%. This is a sustainable strategic win that should be replicated across other regions.

However, this success masks **retail channel underperformance** (-$34K) concentrated in two problem stores (#47 and #12). The wholesale gains are partially offsetting retail losses, creating a false sense of overall performance.

**Actionable Insights:**
1. **Scale wholesale success**: Document distributor onboarding playbook for regional rollout
2. **Address retail issues**: Prioritize Store #47 and #12 remediation (see store deep dives)
3. **Investigate unexplained variance**: $40K requires deeper analysis (possible data quality issue or missed opportunity)

### Gross Margin Variance: Q1 2026

**Variance:** -2.3% vs. Target

#### Variance Decomposition

| Driver | Margin Impact | Root Cause |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| Excessive discounting (Store #12) | -1.2% | Unauthorized discount practices |
| Product mix shift | -0.5% | Higher proportion of lower-margin wholesale sales |
| Supplier cost increases | -0.4% | Inflationary pressure on COGS |
| Inventory obsolescence | -0.2% | Slow-moving inventory at Store #33 |
| **Net Variance** | **-2.3%** | **Fully explained** |

**Business Narrative:**

The margin variance is **not a market-driven issue** but a **controllable operational issue**. The primary driver (-1.2%) is Store #12's excessive discounting, which is immediately addressable through policy changes.

The product mix shift (-0.5%) is a strategic trade-off: accepting lower wholesale margins to gain volume and market share. This should be evaluated as a strategic decision, not a performance failure.

**Actionable Insights:**
1. **Immediate margin recovery**: Implement discount controls at Store #12 (expected +1.0% margin recovery)
2. **Strategic mix review**: Evaluate if wholesale margin targets need adjustment given volume strategy
3. **Supplier negotiation**: Leverage volume increases to negotiate better COGS terms (expected +0.3% margin recovery)

## Business Recommendations by KPI Category

### Sales & Revenue Recommendations

**Based on KPI Analysis:**

1. **Channel Strategy Optimization**
   - **Finding**: Wholesale channel outperforming retail by 23% margin efficiency
   - **Recommendation**: Shift 15% of marketing budget from retail to wholesale channel development
   - **Expected Impact**: +$45K monthly revenue, +1.2% overall margin

2. **Store Performance Tiering**
   - **Finding**: Top 20% of stores generate 55% of revenue; bottom 20% generate 8%
   - **Recommendation**: Implement tiered resource allocation (inventory, marketing, staffing)
   - **Expected Impact**: +$32K monthly revenue through optimized resource deployment

3. **Pricing Strategy**
   - **Finding**: Average discount rate 8.4% but varies 4-18% across stores
   - **Recommendation**: Standardize discount policy with regional manager approval for exceptions
   - **Expected Impact**: +$28K monthly margin improvement

### Inventory & Operations Recommendations

**Based on KPI Analysis:**

1. **Inventory Rebalancing**
   - **Finding**: 15 stores with critical stockouts; 8 stores with excess inventory (>30 days supply)
   - **Recommendation**: Implement inter-store inventory transfer program
   - **Expected Impact**: +$12K monthly sales recovery, $8K monthly inventory carrying cost reduction

2. **Distribution Center Alignment**
   - **Finding**: DC replenishment lagging sales patterns by 5 days on average
   - **Recommendation**: Implement demand-driven replenishment vs. fixed schedule
   - **Expected Impact**: +$18K monthly sales, 25% reduction in stockouts

3. **Assortment Optimization**
   - **Finding**: 40% of SKUs generate <5% of revenue; top 20% generate 65% of revenue
   - **Recommendation**: Rationalize SKU count by 15%, focus on high-velocity items
   - **Expected Impact**: +$22K monthly margin through improved inventory turnover

### Labor & Productivity Recommendations

**Based on KPI Analysis:**

1. **Staffing Optimization**
   - **Finding**: Labor cost % varies 14-22% across stores with no correlation to sales
   - **Recommendation**: Implement demand-based staffing model
   - **Expected Impact**: $38K monthly labor cost reduction, maintained service levels

2. **Productivity Incentives**
   - **Finding**: No correlation between labor hours and sales performance across stores
   - **Recommendation**: Implement sales-per-labor-hour KPI with manager incentives
   - **Expected Impact**: +15% labor productivity, $25K monthly value

3. **Cross-Training Investment**
   - **Finding**: Stores with cross-trained staff show 22% higher labor productivity
   - **Recommendation**: Implement company-wide cross-training program
   - **Expected Impact**: $18K monthly labor efficiency improvement

## Appendix: KPI Definitions Reference

**Key KPIs Used in Analysis:**
- Net Sales: Total revenue after discounts
- Gross Margin %: (Net Sales - COGS) / Net Sales
- In-Stock Rate: % of SKUs with positive inventory
- Sales per Labor Hour: Net Sales / Total Labor Hours
- Labor Cost %: Labor Cost / Net Sales
- Average Discount Rate: Total Discount Amount / Gross Sales
- Inventory Turnover: COGS / Average Inventory Value

**Data Sources:**
- `mart.fact_sales_daily`
- `mart.fact_inventory_snapshot_daily`
- `mart.fact_labor_daily`
- `mart.kpi_gross_margin_daily`
- `mart.kpi_sales_per_labor_hour_daily`
- `mart.kpi_instock_rate_daily`
- `mart.recon_sales_to_gl_monthly`

**Analysis Period:** Q1 2026 (January - March)
**Currency:** USD
**Analysis Method:** SQL-based KPI calculations with business narrative interpretation
